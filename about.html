Biography

I am a Chilean American raised in the Rio Grande Valley of South Texas. I received a BA with major in Asian Studies and a BS in Bioengineering with Distinction in Research and Creative works from Rice University. I received my MS and PhD in the joint Biomedical Engineering program between Georgia Institute of Technology and Emory University.

Currently I am a postdoc in Dr. Elisseeff's lab at the Translational Tissue Engineering Center at Johns Hopkins University. I work on understanding the immune-fibrosis axis in two injuries: a synthetic biomaterial implant in a wound and Uterine Fibroids.

Research Areas

My research interests is the interface of immunology and biomaterials in the context of inflammation, tissue regeneration, and women's health.
